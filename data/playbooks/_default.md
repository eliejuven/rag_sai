# Sector Playbook: General (Default)

## Source
- Faculty/practitioner: none yet — this is a sector-agnostic baseline used
  until a sector-specific playbook exists for the company's CVM sector.
- Date: 2026-06-12

## 1. Top credit risk drivers (sector-agnostic)
- **Cash generation vs. fixed obligations.** Whether operating cash flow
  reliably covers debt service, lease payments, and committed capex matters
  more than the absolute level of earnings — a profitable company can still
  be a weak credit if cash is tied up in working capital or capex.
- **Leverage trajectory, not the snapshot.** Is Net Debt / EBITDA (as
  disclosed) trending up or down across the available years, and *why*?
  Deleveraging from EBITDA growth is a different story than deleveraging
  from asset sales or equity issuance.
- **Refinancing / maturity concentration.** A company can look fine on
  leverage ratios today and still face acute risk if a large share of debt
  matures in the next 12-24 months, especially under a high domestic policy
  rate (Selic) — check FRE 1.15 / debt notes for maturity profile.

## 2. Common analyst mistakes / oversimplifications
- Treating a company's disclosed "Adjusted EBITDA" or other non-GAAP figure
  (FRE 2.5) as directly comparable across companies — or across periods for
  the *same* company — without checking the stated definition. Add-backs
  vary widely and can change between reporting periods.
- Reading a single period's ratio in isolation instead of the trend across
  all available DFP/ITR years. One strong or weak quarter is not a trend.
- Focusing on income-statement profitability while ignoring working-capital
  swings (receivables, inventory, payables) that drive — or destroy — actual
  cash generation.
- Ignoring qualitative disclosures (contingencies, related-party
  transactions, contractual commitments/guarantees in FRE 1.15 / 4.x) that
  don't appear as BPP line items but represent real claims on future cash
  flow.

## 3. Signals: "good" vs "concerning"
| Signal | Looks healthy when... | Red flag when... |
|---|---|---|
| Leverage (Net Debt / disclosed EBITDA) | Stable or declining across the available years | Rising for 2+ consecutive periods, especially alongside margin compression |
| Liquidity (cash + short-term investments vs. short-term debt) | Cash comfortably covers the next 12 months of maturities | Short-term debt materially exceeds cash with no committed credit lines disclosed |
| Margin trend | Gross/operating margins stable or improving | Margins compressing while revenue grows ("growth at any cost") |
| Working capital | Receivables/inventory roughly track revenue growth | Receivables or inventory growing materially faster than revenue |
| Contingencies (FRE 4.x) | Provisioned amounts stable; "probable" losses are provisioned | Large "possible" (not "probable") contingencies that would be material relative to equity if realized |
| Governance / related parties (FRE 1.12/5.3/6.5/7.1) | Clear, arm's-length terms; stable board/ownership structure | Material related-party transactions with thin disclosure, or frequent auditor/management changes |

## 4. Sector-agnostic metrics / framings
- Net Debt / disclosed EBITDA (note whether using a company-defined
  "Adjusted EBITDA" and its definition, or a DRE-derived equivalent)
- Interest coverage: disclosed EBITDA / financial expenses (DRE)
- Cash conversion: operating cash flow (DFC) vs. net income, across periods
- Revenue and margin trend across all available DFP/ITR years
- Debt maturity concentration, where disclosed

## 5. Characteristic reasoning / language
- **Lead with the trend, not the level**: "Leverage is moderate at 2.1x, but
  has risen from 1.4x two years ago — driven primarily by [X], which
  [is/isn't] likely to reverse."
- **Tie qualitative disclosures to the numbers**: "The risk-factors section
  flags [Y] as material; this is consistent with [metric] showing [Z]."
- **Be explicit about gaps**: "No FRE coverage of [section] is available for
  this company, so [topic] cannot be assessed from disclosed sources alone."
- **Distinguish disclosed non-GAAP from GAAP figures**: "the company reports
  Adjusted EBITDA of R$X (defined as [...]), compared to a DRE-derived
  EBITDA-equivalent of R$Y."
- **Avoid false precision**: round appropriately, and flag when a figure is
  approximate or derived rather than directly stated by the company.
